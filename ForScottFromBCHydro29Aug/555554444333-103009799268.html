Did you know?
Your rate has two steps
You're on the Residential Conservation Rate (Rate Schedule 1101). This means you're charged one rate for electricity up to a threshold (calculated by 22.1918 kWh per day multiplied by the number of days in the billing period), and a higher rate for all electricity beyond that threshold.
This rate structure is designed to encourage conservation and is referred to as a "stepped rate". The first portion is called Step 1 and the amount above that is called Step 2.

Common questions
 
How do I pay my bill?
There are a number of ways to pay your bill.
·Make an online payment at bchydro.com using MyHydro
·Auto-pay
·Online banking
·Pay by credit card (using a third-party service that charges a service fee for each payment you make)
·Pay by mail
·Pay at a Service BC location (outside the Lower Mainland)
Learn more about the ways to pay your bill.
 
Is there a way to budget my bill payments?
You can set-up an Equal Payment Plan to make equal payments each month. The last 12 months of electricity use determines your monthly payment amount.
Each year, we compare the amount you've been billed with your actual electricity use. The difference may result in a credit (if you've used less electricity than you've paid for) or additional charges (if you've been using more electricity than you've paid for). You can see how you're comparing on each bill.
 
How do I conserve electricity?
There are a number of ways to conserve electricity, which help to reduce your bill.
Residential customers can:
·Green your home with our no-cost tips for saving electricity and money.
·Do it yourself
·Check out our savings and rebates to help you save.
·Join Team Power Smart, activate a Challenge and earn $50.
For business customers, whether your business is a grocery store, restaurant or a large pulp and paper mill, we offer programs to help you improve your energy efficiency and save money
 
 
How do I contact BC Hydro?
You can get many of your questions answered on our website.
You can also call us at 1 800 BCHYDRO (1 800 224 9376). We're available Monday to Friday from 7 a.m. to 8 p.m. and Saturday from 9 a.m. to 5 p.m.
Some of our busiest call times are Monday through Friday between 9 a.m. and noon, and 4 p.m. to 6 p.m. You may have a shorter wait time if you give us a call outside of these times.

Late payment charges
The full amount owing on your bill is 21 days after the billing date. If we receive your payment after this date, a late payment charge of 1.5% per month (19.56% per year) of your entire outstanding balance will be added to your account if your unpaid balance is over $30. Learn more.
