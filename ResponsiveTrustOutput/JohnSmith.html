Annuity Contract Terminology
An annuity contract is a contractual obligation between as many as four parties. They are the issuer (us), the owner of the annuity (you), the annuitant, and the beneficiary.
The annuity owner is the person who buys an annuity.
An annuitant is the individual whose life is used as the yardstick for determining when benefits payments will start and cease. In most cases, though not all, the owner and annuitant are the same person.
The beneficiary is the individual designated by the annuity owner who will receive any death benefit when the annuitant dies.
